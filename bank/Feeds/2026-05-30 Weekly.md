# Weekly Briefing — 2026-05-30

## This Week in Craft & Fashion

### Summer Fabrics — the Category Is Hot
Heddels running multiple summer fabric pieces simultaneously: Iron Heart cotton/linen chambray (65% cotton, 35% linen, 7oz, indigo warp/grey linen weft), GUSTIN 6oz featherweight broken twill, Indigofera printed seersucker shirt. The summer shirting category is clearly where editorial energy is going right now. Web trend data confirms seersucker framed as the alternative to linen — "linen out, seersucker in" is a real conversation in SS2026 coverage.

### French Workwear Has a Moment
Long John ran a piece on L'Usine Bleue — a Stockholm store specialising in authentic French workwear brands manufactured in France. Coltin jackets in 350g/m² cotton drill, heavyweight moleskin jackets. Very relevant to our pending [[Ideas/Bleu de Travail - France's Own Workwear Tradition]] idea.

### HBT Reproduction — Headlight
Heddels: Headlight (Toyo Enterprise) released a stitch-for-stitch 1930s reproduction in 10oz cotton herringbone twill. Period-correct bar tacks, chin strap on chore coat, buckle back on pants, paper flashers. HBT (herringbone twill) has deep military origins — the weave structure resists snags via diagonal interlacing. Nobody has written the HBT deep dive.

### Japan — Tsuyu (Rainy Season) Theme
Houyhnhnm is running rainy-season content: Clarks Wallabee Gore-Tex new colours, Crockett & Jones penny loafers for wet weather. Tsuyu (the Japanese rainy season) starts early June — 6 weeks of humidity and rain. The way Japan dresses for tsuyu is a genuinely different approach to wet-weather dressing vs the Western waxed cotton tradition.

### LVC WWII 1944 Reissue
Levi's Vintage Clothing reissued their 1944 WWII model with faithful period details. Japanese denim reproduction community always reacts to LVC drops. Connects to our existing research on wartime denim.

### Silk Shirts — Oversized
Houyhnhnm: "シルクシャツは身幅大きめが気分" — silk shirts worn oversized is the current mood in Tokyo. New Vintage series framing it as relaxed, not formal. Interesting counterpoint to the workwear-heavy content Crafta has been running.
