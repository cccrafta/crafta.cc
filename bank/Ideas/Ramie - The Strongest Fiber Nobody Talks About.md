## Angle
5000 years old, outperforms cotton in every metric, nearly forgotten by modern fashion. Ramie is a bast fiber with exceptional tensile strength, natural luster, and resistance to bacteria and mildew, yet it remains one of the most overlooked textiles in contemporary garment making.
