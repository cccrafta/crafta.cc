## Research Notes
From Harrington research: Grenfell cloth = 600 threads-per-inch Egyptian cotton gabardine invented 1923, "permeable to perspiration." Ventile = super-long cotton fibers, tight weave, designed to save pilots in icy water. Original Baracuta was high-thread-count cotton; modern = 50/50 poly-cotton. Forum discussions suggest Grenfell may be higher quality than modern Baracuta.

## Angle
Technical deep dive with fabric specs, breathability measurements, water resistance testing. What's marketing vs actual performance? Does poly-cotton actually improve the jacket or just reduce cost? Can you feel the difference between 600-thread Egyptian cotton and modern blends?
