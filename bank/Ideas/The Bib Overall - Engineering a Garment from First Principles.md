## The Idea

The bib overall is one of the most over-looked garments in workwear writing because it's treated as obvious. It's not. Every single feature was engineered to solve a specific failure point:

- **Bib**: originally a convertible working surface (1896 patent) — outer bib folds down as a tool apron, folds up as chest coverage
- **High back/continuous back**: prevents the gap between waist and back that bench work creates
- **Swinging pockets**: pockets that pivot at the waist seam so they don't bunch when bending
- **Bar tacks**: reinforce the specific points where pockets, seams, and suspender attachments fail
- **Riveted buttons**: replace sewn buttons that tear under strain
- **Crotch reinforcement** (1890): a single fabric blank serving as both fly closure and back seam reinforcement — no added piece, no ridge

## Primary Sources

**US432669 (1890, Baltimore)** — Hohman & Cunningham crotch reinforcement patent
**US564373 (1896, Chicago)** — Goodman convertible bib patent  
**US1152289A (1915, Detroit)** — Hamilton Carhartt himself, systematic reinforcement
**1929 Sears Roebuck Catalogue** — "INDESTRUCTIBLE POCKETS — wearproof — guaranteed for the life of the garment. A year of experimenting proved successful."

## Engineering Timeline
- 1890: Crotch/fly reinforcement solved
- 1896: Convertible working bib invented
- 1911: H.D. Lee invents the modern bib overall form
- 1914: Carhartt patents systematic reinforcement  
- 1929: Sears markets "Hercules" overalls in "Double Service" 8-oz pre-shrunk denim with lifetime seam guarantee
- 1930: Sanforization invented (replaces "pre-shrunk by water and steam")
