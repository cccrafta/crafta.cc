## The Idea

Every heritage garment nerd obsesses over fabric weight and construction but ignores the zipper — which is itself a piece of precision engineering with a specific history. Key threads:

- **Origin**: Whitcomb Judson's 1893 "clasp locker" for shoes, not garments. The modern zipper design by Gideon Sundback in 1913.
- **Workwear adoption**: Zippers replaced buttons on work jackets and coveralls because buttons catch on machinery and fail under repeated industrial strain.
- **The hardware wars**: Talon (dominant 1920s-1940s), Crown, Conmar, Scovill — each had different tooth geometry and slider mechanisms. Reproduction houses obsess over which zipper a specific vintage garment used.
- **YKK**: Yoshida Kogyo Kabushiki Kaisha, founded 1934, Osaka. Now makes about half of all zippers worldwide. Why did a Japanese company come to dominate global zipper production?
- **Vintage-spec details**: The tooth profile, pull shape, and slider mechanism of a Talon 42 vs a modern YKK are different. Why reproduction brands like Buzz Rickson's and The Real McCoy's spec specific vintage hardware.
