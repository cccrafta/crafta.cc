## The Idea

No garment has a more documented origin story than the American flight jacket. Every iteration was defined by military specification numbers:

- **A-1 (1927)**: First standardized flight jacket. Knit collar and cuffs, snap front. Summer use.
- **A-2 (1931)**: The iconic leather flight jacket. Specified horsehide (later goatskin). Particular collar construction, specific zipper hardware (Crown or Talon), knit cuffs. Modified repeatedly through WWII.
- **G-1 (Navy)**: Similar to A-2 but with fur collar. Navy specification.
- **B-3 (Army Air Corps)**: Shearling for high-altitude cold. The "bomber jacket" shape.
- **MA-1 (1950s)**: Nylon replaced leather as jets replaced propellers. The orange lining (for visibility if downed). Ribbed collar instead of structured.

**The reproduction angle**: Buzz Rickson's, The Real McCoy's, and Aero Leather have spent decades tracking down original spec sheets, interviewing veterans, and sourcing period-correct hardware to make accurate reproductions. The paper trail is extraordinary — military contracts specify everything.

**The garment as document**: Each design change in the flight jacket corresponds to a specific operational need. When jets flew higher and faster, the leather A-2 became inadequate (too rigid in extreme cold, not fire-resistant). The MA-1's nylon was the engineering response.
