## Angle
How scarcity-driven textile repair became an aspirational design language — from boro to Kapital to runway. What began as a necessity in rural Japan, where cotton was too precious to discard and garments were patched and reinforced across generations, has been transformed into a deliberate aesthetic embraced by high fashion and heritage brands alike.
