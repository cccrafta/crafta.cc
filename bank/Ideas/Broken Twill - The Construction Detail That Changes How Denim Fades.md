## Angle
Broken twill is one of those construction details that sounds technical but reveals something interesting about design intentionality. Standard denim's "leg twist" is a consequence of the weave structure, not a design choice — it was just how twill wove. Broken twill was developed to eliminate an unintended effect. The Wrangler 13MWZ (1964) was the first commercial broken twill denim. GUSTIN's featherweight broken twill (May 2026) brings it to a lightweight context. Technique post: explain the weave, the twist problem, the solution, what it means for how a garment ages.

## Key Facts
- Standard twill: warp threads pass over multiple weft threads in one direction → fabric torques on the diagonal → jeans "twist" and leg rotates over time
- Broken twill: direction reverses periodically → diagonal stresses cancel out → no leg rotation
- First commercial broken twill denim: Wrangler 13MWZ, 1964
- GUSTIN featherweight broken twill (2026): making the construction accessible at lower price point
