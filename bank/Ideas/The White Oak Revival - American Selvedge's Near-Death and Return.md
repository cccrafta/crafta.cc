## Angle
The White Oak story is about what happens when industrial knowledge almost disappears. The Draper X3 looms are machines no longer manufactured; the weavers who ran them are aging; the cotton preparation and finishing techniques are embedded in people, not manuals. Mount Vernon acquiring the looms is necessary but not sufficient — they also need to reconstruct the knowledge. That reconstruction is the story.

## Key Facts
- Cone Mills White Oak plant closed December 2017 — last large-scale selvedge weaving in America
- 45 Draper X3 shuttle looms acquired by Mount Vernon Mills via KaKa Cotton partnership
- Relocated to Mount Vernon's facility in Trion, Georgia
- First rolls of American selvedge expected "in weeks" per Bill Rogers (Mount Vernon president)
- Same deal includes 45 Picanol President shuttle looms — potential for Cramerton Army Cloth revival
