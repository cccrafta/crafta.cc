## Angle
The "only Japan makes heritage denim" narrative is outdated. Indonesian makers like Bluesville use entirely natural dyestuffs — indigo alongside mangosteen, persimmons, and jengkol bark — producing colors Japanese brands don't attempt. Sage was first to commercially produce 22oz denim. These aren't knockoff scenes; they're independent craft traditions that grew out of a similar reverence for American workwear originals. The Cultural angle: what does it mean when craft traditions replicate across geographies? What do they preserve and what do they change?

## Key Brands
- **Oldblue Co.** (Indonesia) — Americana repro, WWII-era Levi's, cinch-backs
- **Sage** (Indonesia) — First 22oz commercial denim, heavyweight specialists
- **Bluesville** (Indonesia) — All natural dyestuffs: indigo, mangosteen, persimmons, jengkol
- **Piger Works** (Thailand) — Founded 2009, selvedge specialists
- **Nama Denim** (Thailand) — Japanese mills, vintage machines in downtown Bangkok
