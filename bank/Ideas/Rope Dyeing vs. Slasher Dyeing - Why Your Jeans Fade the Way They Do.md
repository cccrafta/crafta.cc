## Angle
The industrial process that determines whether your denim develops high-contrast fades or stays uniformly dark. Rope dyeing dips twisted yarn ropes through indigo baths repeatedly, coating only the outer surface and leaving the core white, which is precisely why rope-dyed denim fades dramatically at stress points while slasher-dyed denim retains a more even color.
