## Angle
The coverall is an underwritten garment. It predates the chore coat, the work jacket, and the overall as a unified solution. Different industries developed different versions — railroad, mechanic, painter, farmer — each with trade-specific pocket placement and reinforcement. The Japanese interpretation (oRslow, others) treats it as a craft object rather than a utility item. The linen version introduces fading as a material characteristic: the yellowing and lightening of linen over time is not degradation, it's documentation.
