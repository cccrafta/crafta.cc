## Content

<p>The CPO — Chief Petty Officer — shirt was a heavy wool overshirt issued to U.S. Navy non-commissioned officers. It occupied a specific niche in the military wardrobe: too heavy to be a regular shirt, too light to be a proper jacket. This in-between quality is precisely what makes it useful.</p>
<p>The original CPO shirt was made from a dense melton wool in navy blue, with a button front, two chest pockets with button flaps, and a straight hem that could be worn tucked or untucked. The fabric was heavy enough to provide genuine warmth but soft enough to wear comfortably as a shirt layer.</p>
<p>As a layering piece, the CPO occupies territory that few other garments cover effectively. It&#8217;s the layer between a shirt and a coat — warm enough for a cool morning, light enough to stuff in a bag when the sun comes out. The shirt collar means it works under a jacket without the bulk of a sweater.</p>
<p>Modern versions in brushed flannel, heavyweight chamois, or quilted cotton expand the concept while maintaining the essential idea: a shirt that does the work of a light jacket. The proportions matter — a CPO should be slightly oversized through the body to accommodate layers beneath it, but not so large that it loses the clean lines of a shirt.</p>
