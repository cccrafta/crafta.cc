# Framework Improvements to Revisit

These gaps were identified after writing the CPO Shirt, Breton Stripe, and Safari Jacket rewrites. Revisit after Phase 2 (all posts at consistent quality) when we have more concrete examples to draw from.

## 1. Stance Section — Too Abstract
The current Stance section describes what good stances feel like but doesn't help produce them. The best closings we've written emerged from the specific narrative, not from the framework's guidance. Need to add practical methods for arriving at a stance — possibly with examples from our strongest closings (moleskin, safari jacket, invisible layer).

## 2. Length Guidance — Too Rigid
"4-5 standard, 5-7 deep dive" doesn't match reality. The Safari Jacket ended up at 7 paragraphs because the subject demanded it. Should replace word/paragraph counts with a principle: the right length is whatever the subject demands, no more, no less.

## 3. What NOT to Write About
No guidance on when a topic doesn't deserve its own post. Some topics might be better as a paragraph within another post. Need criteria for when to write, when to merge, when to skip.

## 4. Revision Process
The framework describes first-draft writing but not revision. Our best posts went through 3-4 rounds. Need to codify: what to look for when re-reading, how to identify weak passages (vague assertions, unsupported claims, disconnected paragraphs), and when a post is done.

## 5. Research to Writing Ratio
Research notes (bank/Research/) are often 10x longer than the posts. The framework says "details serve the narrative" but doesn't explicitly guide what to include vs. leave in the research note. Need a decision framework for cutting.

## Examples to Draw From
When revisiting, reference these posts as case studies:
- **Safari Jacket (51/60)** — strongest rewrite, shows revision value (material criticality paragraph, contemporary makers, Hemingway bespoke details all came from feedback rounds)
- **Moleskin (54/60)** — strongest original, shows framework at full strength
- **Invisible Layer (50/60)** — shows narrative coherence done well
- **Breton Stripe (45/60)** — shows cultural icons done right (Picasso quote, Gaultier substance)
