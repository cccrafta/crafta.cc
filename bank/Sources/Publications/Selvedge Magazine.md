## Overview

Selvedge is a London-based textile arts publication founded in 2004. Billed as "the fabric of your life," it covers cloth, culture, and creativity from around the world — from Bengali jamdani weaving to Scottish tweed mills to Japanese katazome dyeing. The blog posts daily; the print magazine is bimonthly.

## Editorial Perspective

More academic and arts-oriented than Heddels or Permanent Style, but not dry. Coverage includes textile artist profiles, exhibition reviews, fiber history deep dives, and maker interviews. Global scope — not just Western heritage traditions. Strong on non-Western textile traditions (Indian, African, Japanese, Central Asian).
