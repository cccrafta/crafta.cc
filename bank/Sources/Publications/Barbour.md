## About
Founded 1894 by John Barbour in South Shields, North East England. Perfected paraffin wax process for cotton. Three Royal Warrants. Over 60,000 jackets re-waxed annually. The longest-running relationship with waxed cotton in clothing.

T1 primary source for anything involving waxed cotton, field clothing, or British heritage outerwear. They made the jackets — their account of construction, materials, and history is authoritative. Heddels' Barbour brand profile is excellent but secondary to Barbour's own records.
