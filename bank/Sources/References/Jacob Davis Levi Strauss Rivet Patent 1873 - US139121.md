## Summary
The foundational patent in denim history. Filed August 9, 1872 by Jacob W. Davis (Reno, Nevada). Granted May 20, 1873. Assignee: Jacob W. Davis and Levi Strauss & Co. (San Francisco). Describes riveted trouser pocket openings. This is NOT bib overalls — this is the riveted waist-high trouser (what we now call jeans) with button-attached suspenders but no bib.

## Key Claims
- **The problem**: "The seams are usually ripped or started by the placing of the hands in the pockets and the consequent pressure or strain upon them"
- **The solution**: Metal rivets or eyelets at each edge of pocket openings, passed through a hole at the end of the seam, "bind the two parts of cloth together, and then headed down upon both sides"
- **Effect**: Transfers stress from thread (which breaks under strain) to metal (which doesn't)
- **Origin story**: Davis used copper rivets from a horse blanket for a customer whose husband kept tearing work pants. Could not afford the $68 patent fee alone, wrote to Levi Strauss proposing to split it
- **Filed**: August 9, 1872 — granted May 20, 1873
- **This is "waist overalls"** (jeans) with suspenders — the bib was NOT invented until 1911 by H.D. Lee
- Materials: Only "fabric" and "cloth" specified generically
