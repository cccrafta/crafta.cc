## Summary
Astaire's fitting process at Anderson & Sheppard: he would dance up and down the hall during try-ons to check fit. High armhole skimming the armpit, collar and back-neck cut close for maximum stability during movement. Astaire famously threw new suits against walls to remove stiffness.

## Key Claims
- Astaire danced during fittings to test fit
- High armhole for movement
- "English drape" soft tailoring — comfortable as pyjamas while looking formal
- Astaire threw new suits against walls to break stiffness
