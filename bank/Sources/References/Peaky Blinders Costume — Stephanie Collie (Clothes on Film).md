## Summary
Primary source interview with Stephanie Collie, Season 1 costume designer for Peaky Blinders. Covers research methodology, specific construction decisions, sourcing, and the philosophy of historical heightening for contemporary audiences.

## Key Claims
- Research sources: Getty Images archives and the book *Crooks Like Us* by Peter Doyle (hairstyle inspiration)
- The real Peaky Blinders gang predated the show's timeline by approximately 20 years
- Tommy Shelby's suits: bespoke, made by Keith Watson (Savile Row-trained specialist)
- Supporting cast suits: hired from Angels and Cosprop, then tailored with narrower trouser legs
- Some overcoats sourced as vintage originals; others custom-made
- Shirts: hired from costume houses
- Stiff collars: Collie insisted on tight shirt collars worn close to the neck despite actor discomfort
- "We heighten things to make them more relatable" — explicit statement of approach
- Women's costumes: many pieces custom-made based on original period designs
- Silk stockings used for female characters (noted as delicate during muddy filming)
- One fur coat: original 1920s piece in excellent condition
