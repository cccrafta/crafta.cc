## Summary
Comprehensive safari jacket buying guide covering history, design features, Hollywood adoption, and styling advice.

## Key Claims
- Safari jacket "invented at the end of the 19th century" but exact roots remain unclear
- Clark Gable wore bush jackets in Red Dust (1932) and Mogambo (1953)
- Roger Moore wore safari jackets as Bond in The Man with the Golden Gun, Octopussy, and Moonraker
- YSL introduced the "Saharienne" collection in 1968
- Original bush jacket material was lightweight tan cotton twill, later made from poplin
- Defining features: 4 symmetrical flapped front pockets, belt, and epaulettes
