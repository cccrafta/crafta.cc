## Summary
Primary source account of Round House Manufacturing's founding and history. Founded 1903 in Shawnee, Oklahoma (then Indian Territory, 4 years before Oklahoma statehood). Named for the "round house" railroad repair station. The oldest operating manufacturing company in Oklahoma and the largest remaining American manufacturer of bib overalls.

## Key Claims
- **Founded 1903** — Shawnee, Oklahoma (Indian Territory, 4 years before statehood)
- **Named for** the railroad "round house" repair station — over 100 trains passed through Shawnee daily at founding
- **Original customers**: railroad workers building toward American westward expansion
- **Historical context**: Spanish-American War recently ended, Teddy Roosevelt president, Wright brothers flying that year, average American worked 10 hours/day, 6 days/week for $12/week
- **Current production**: ~300,000 units/year, 25,000 pairs/month, 1,250 pairs/day, 12 million feet of thread/month
- **Status**: Largest manufacturer of American-made jeans and overalls today
- **Motto**: "Fit Best, Wear Longest" — unchanged since founding
- Still uses Oklahoma-grown cotton for some products
