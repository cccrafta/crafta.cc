## Summary
Profile of Josh Guillory, an architectural blacksmith in Lake Charles, Louisiana, who opened Iron Shop Provisions in 2018 — a heritage menswear shop in the front showroom of his welding workshop. Reveals the gap between heritage denim's workwear identity and its actual customer base.

## Key Claims
- Josh Guillory is an architectural blacksmith who welded staircases and chandeliers
- Discovered Japanese denim culture via Instagram hashtags around 2010
- Opened Iron Shop Provisions in 2018 in the front of his welding workshop in Lake Charles, LA
- "90 percent of our customers, if I had to guess, don't ride motorcycles or weld. The guys that we communicate with through DMs and feedback are working in a cubicle"
- His own brand A New Medium (ANM) launched 2023 with a denim jacket and Hainesworth wool overshirt
- ANM uses 8oz Japanese loopwheel T-shirts, sashiko button-downs, 14oz and 20oz Japanese Kaizen denim
- Fabrics sourced in Japan, jeans sewn in Los Angeles
- Customers "look the part, and they feel good about it" despite not doing manual labor
