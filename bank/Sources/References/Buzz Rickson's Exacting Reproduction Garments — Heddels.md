## Summary
Brand profile of Buzz Rickson's covering Toyo Enterprise's origins, the Japan-to-America-to-Japan pipeline of military garment culture, the brand's reproduction methodology, and the William Gibson collaboration. Explains why Japan became the global leader in military reproduction clothing.

## Key Claims
- Buzz Rickson's parent company Toyo Enterprises began as Kosho & Co. in the 1940s
- Buzz Rickson's brand itself established in 1993 in Japan
- Kosho & Co. initially manufactured garments sold to American servicemen stationed in Japan after WWII, through Korean and Vietnam war eras, into the 1970s — making Hawaiian shirts and souvenir jackets for American GIs
- "They were actually born out of the period they work so hard to emulate" — the Japan–America military cultural exchange is direct, not secondhand
- Brand named after Steve McQueen's aviator character Buzz Rickson in the 1961 film The War Lover
- Reproduction methodology: infrared spectroscopy to match thread colors, vintage machinery restored, materials analyzed to molecular level
- William Gibson novel Pattern Recognition featured fictional all-black MA-1 by Buzz Rickson's — reader demand created actual all-black "William Gibson line"
- Buzz Rickson's didn't reach U.S. markets until 2003
- CPO shirt: 100% melton wool, made in Japan on vintage looms; wool boiled then sun-dried for soft, non-scratchy hand
