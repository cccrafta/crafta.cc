## Summary
Educational overview of luxury wool types focusing on cashmere, lambswool, angora, and others — covering fiber origin, fineness, yield, and garment care.

## Key Claims
- Cashmere obtained from undercoat of cashmere goat; undercoat makes up about one-quarter of total fleece
- Fiber diameter approximately 18 microns (similar to superfine merino)
- Finest cashmere comes from neck region of undercoat
- One goat typically yields 150 grams of cashmere per year — low output explains high price
- Combing takes one to two weeks per animal
- Cashmere has similar resilience to sheep's wool but finer fibers make it more delicate
- Cashmere is a hair fiber (from goat), not wool (from sheep) — distinction matters for care and fiber behavior
