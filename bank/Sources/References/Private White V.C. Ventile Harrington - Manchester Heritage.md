## Summary
Primary source documentation of Private White V.C.'s Ventile Harrington jacket, made in Manchester in the same city where Baracuta originated. Uses WWII-era Ventile fabric invented for RAF pilots, maintaining Manchester manufacturing tradition.

## Key Claims
- **Made in Manchester, England** — handmade in Private White V.C.'s own factory
- **Materials sourced in United Kingdom** — almost exclusively UK-sourced materials
- **Ventile fabric origin**: Invented during WWII when Winston Churchill called for fabric to protect downed RAF pilots in freezing waters
- **Ventile specifications**: 100% cotton at 205 grams per square metre, 100% weatherproof, highly breathable and windproof
- **Military-grade hardware**: Copper components from RIRI of Switzerland
- **Named after Private Jack White V.C.** — who won Victoria Cross in WWI
- **Heritage claim**: "First stitched in the 1950s and still made in Manchester"
- **Cultural positioning**: "dressed rebels, musicians, and silver-screen legends for generations"
- **Available colors**: Midnight Navy, British Racing Green, Black, among others
- **Production location**: Manchester factory, continuing the city's garment-making tradition
