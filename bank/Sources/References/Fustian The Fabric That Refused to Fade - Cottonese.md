## Summary
Overview of the fustian family — corduroy, moleskin, velveteen. Covers the shared construction (weft floats cut to create pile), Al-Fustat origins (~200 CE), and how each member differs in finishing.

## Key Claims
- Fustian derives from Al-Fusṭāṭ, ancient capital of Egypt near Cairo
- Originally linen warp, cotton weft — manufactured as early as 200 CE
- Fustian defines fabric by construction: weft floats over multiple warp threads
- Moleskin: brushed nap, no ribs
- Corduroy: cut pile in visible ridges (wales)
- Velveteen: very fine ridges (32 per inch), resembles velvet
- British Chartists wore fustian jackets as class allegiance symbol in 19th century
