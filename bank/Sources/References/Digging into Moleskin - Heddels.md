## Summary
Comprehensive history and construction overview of moleskin fabric. Covers fustian classification, satin weave construction, napping/peaching process, medieval origins, and transition from workwear to fashion.

## Key Claims
- Moleskin is technically a fustian fabric (same family as corduroy and velveteen)
- Tightly woven with a satin weave, both warp and weft same color
- One side is shorn/brushed — process called "peaching" (like fuzz on a peach)
- Origins trace to medieval Europe: farmers and hunters needing warmth, durability, wind resistance
- Industrialization made production more accessible — adopted by steelworkers, factory laborers
- Cord was to England what moleskin was to France
- Modern revival via vintage French workwear and brands like Le Laboureur, Le Mont St. Michel, Vetra
