## Summary
Detailed breakdown of Baracuta G9 construction features and manufacturing process, with emphasis on umbrella back yoke design and current UK/European production.

## Key Claims

### Umbrella Back Yoke Design
- **Inspiration**: "Distinctive shape of the back yoke was inspired by the ubiquitous rain repellent umbrella"
- **Function**: "Back yoke is designed to get rid of the rain without compromising the overall look"
- **Miller brothers innovation**: "Ingenuously came up with the umbrella back yoke with a built-in vent for a more breathable garment"
- **Also called**: "Wing Back Umbrella" or "Back Yoke" — offers wearer "added flare of movement and structure"
- **Original purpose**: Specifically for golfers dealing with rainy English weather

### Manufacturing
- **Made in UK**: "Crafted in the UK by proficient British craftsmen"
- **Heritage claim**: "Designed and made in the UK since 1937"
- **Current production**: "Made in England, though production now takes place mainly in Europe, including Italy, depending on the specific style"
- **DNA continuity**: "Jacket to this day still holds the DNA from when it first began"
- **Standards**: "Fabrics and construction follow the brand's original standards, with close attention to fit, durability, and finishing"

### Construction Features
- **Fraser Tartan lining**: Made from breathable Coolmax® fabric (modern update from original cotton tartan)
- **Two-button dog ear collar**: Signature feature
- **Ribbed cuffs and waistband**: Elasticated for blouson fit
- **Deep slanted pockets**: "Designed to hold two golf balls"
- **Baracuta Cloth fabric**: Proprietary water-resistant material
