## Summary
History of Willis & Geiger Outfitters from founding to closure.

## Key Claims
- 1902: Ben Willis developed clothing for his own Arctic explorations
- Sold through Abercrombie & Fitch, which outfitted Teddy Roosevelt with Willis-designed clothing
- 1928: Willis brought in marketer Howard W. Geiger; company founded as Willis & Geiger Outfitters
- 1931: US defense contract for air force pilot jackets
- Outfitted: Roald Amundsen, Charles Lindbergh, Amelia Earhart, Jacqueline Cochran, Sir Edmund Hillary, Jim Fowler, the Flying Tigers
- 1936: Hemingway designed bespoke bush jacket with W&G
- 1977: When Abercrombie & Fitch went bankrupt, W&G was its largest unpaid creditor and went out of business
- Revived by former A&F employee Burt Avedon
- 1986: Sold to VF Corporation, which shut down US manufacturing
- 1987: Sold to Laura Ashley, then to Japanese market
- Today: vintage W&G pieces are highly sought collector's items
