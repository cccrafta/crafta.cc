## Summary
Documentation of contemporary wearers and film appearances of Harrington jackets from 2000s onward, showing continued cultural relevance beyond vintage Hollywood icons.

## Key Claims

### Daniel Craig / James Bond
- **Quantum of Solace (2008)**: Daniel Craig wore Tom Ford Harrington (navy version) in Bolivia desert scenes
- **Tom Ford design**: Slimmer cut, inspired by Baracuta G4 (not G9) according to Bond Lifestyle
- **Also available in cream**: Tom Ford made second version in cream for Daniel Craig
- **Personal life**: Craig spotted with sand-colored Baracuta G9 off-screen, "often inspired by Steve McQueen's sartorial style"
- **Cultural continuity**: Links vintage McQueen cool to modern Bond aesthetic

### Other Contemporary Wearers
- **Jason Statham**: Wore sand-colored Baracuta G9 in *Killer Elite* (2011)
- **Bradley Cooper**: Wore blue Baracuta G9 in *The Hangover Part 2*
- **Christopher Reeve**: Wore Baracuta G9 in *Superman*

### Historical Icons (Reconfirmed)
- **Frank Sinatra and Steve McQueen**: "So often photographed in the jacket in both their movies and personal lives that they could almost be considered unofficial ambassadors of the Harrington"
- **James Dean**: McGregor Drizzler (American counterpart) in *Rebel Without a Cause* (1955)
- **Elvis Presley**: "Blouson" jacket in *King Creole* (1958) considered iconic

### Cultural Observation
List of wearers spanning seven decades: "Elvis Presley, Steve McQueen, Frank Sinatra, Daniel Craig, and Jason Statham have all worn the Baracuta G9 Harrington jacket" — demonstrates sustained cultural relevance from 1950s to present
