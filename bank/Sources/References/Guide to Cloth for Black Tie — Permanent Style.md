## Summary
Comprehensive guide to dinner jacket fabrics. Covers barathea (satin weave, dense, deep drape), wool/mohair blends (crispness, lustre, shape-holding), weight recommendations (9-10oz), and the midnight blue vs black debate.

## Key Claims
- Barathea is a satin-group weave, considered the norm for evening wear in Britain
- 9-10oz is the recommended weight for dinner jackets (heated venues)
- Midnight blue reads as true black under artificial light; actual black reads brownish
- Body cloth should be matte to contrast with lustrous silk lapel facings
- Mohair adds crispness and wrinkle resistance even at low percentages (14-16%)
