## Summary
Traces the safari jacket's origins not to Africa but to British India. The 1842 Afghan disaster showed colourful uniforms were liabilities. Lieutenant Hodson introduced khaki (Persian for "dust") uniforms for the Corps of Guides in Lahore in 1846. The military pattern became the "Shikar Jacket" (shikar = Hindi/Urdu for hunting).

## Key Claims
- 1842: Afghan disaster — colourful uniforms made soldiers vulnerable to snipers
- ~1846: Lt. Hodson introduces khaki uniforms for Corps of Guides, Lahore
- "Khaki" derives from Persian word for "dust"
- The military jacket became the "Shikar Jacket" — a hunting garment
- Captain Henry Shakespear's 1860 book *The Wild Sports of India* details specifications: ~4 inches below waist, five front pockets for tools/powder/ammunition
- 1850s: Wilkinson & Co of Pall Mall advertised shikar jackets commercially
- The term "safari" gained prominence later through African expeditions
