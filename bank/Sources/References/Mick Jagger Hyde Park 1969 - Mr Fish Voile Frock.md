## Summary
Profile of Michael Fish (Mr Fish), the London designer who defined gender-fluid menswear in the 1960s–70s. Covers the Hyde Park concert outfit made for Mick Jagger and the broader cultural significance of the brand.

## Key Claims
- Michael Fish (1940–2016) opened the menswear shop Mr Fish in 1966 with business partner Barry Sainsbury; known for kipper ties, kaftans, and dresses for men
- The white frock worn by Mick Jagger at the Rolling Stones' Hyde Park concert on July 5, 1969 was designed by Michael Fish; the concert was held 48 hours after Brian Jones died
- The garment was made from white cotton voile — bishop-sleeved, loosely cut, falling to mid-thigh over white trousers; inspired by the fustanella (Greek Evzone ceremonial kilt)
- Attended by approximately 250,000 people; described as "one of the most memorable outfits of the spirit of the Sixties"
- Mr Fish was among the first designers to deliberately put men in dresses; garments also worn by David Bowie
