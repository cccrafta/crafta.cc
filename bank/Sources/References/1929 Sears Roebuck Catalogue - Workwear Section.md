## Summary
Spring/Summer 1929 Sears Roebuck mail-order catalogue, workwear section. Primary source for garment construction language, fabric specifications, pricing, and competitive positioning of overalls and work shirts in the late 1920s — one year before Sanforization was invented.

## Key Claims
- **"Double Service" Denim**: Sears proprietary denim described as "extra heavy closely woven (more threads to the inch) full 8-ounce pre-shrunk, both by water and steam"
- **Pre-shrunk before Sanforization**: "pre-shrunk, both by water and steam" — Sanforization not invented until 1930
- **Hercules brand**: Sears premium private-label overalls, positioned against Carhartt and Lee at $2.00–$2.50
- **"INDESTRUCTIBLE POCKETS — wearproof — guaranteed for the life of the garment. A year of experimenting proved successful"**
- **"Seams Guaranteed not to Rip — New garment if they do"** — lifetime seam guarantee
- **Standard construction vocabulary**: "triple stitched seams, bar tacked at all strain points, riveted brass buttons, swinging pockets, continuous facings, adjustable suspenders"
- **Fabric weights**: 8-oz for premium (Hercules), 2:20 weight for mid-grade, various grades described
- **Hickory stripe** mentioned as summer alternative to indigo blue denim
- **Children's overalls**: same construction standards, 53¢ entry price
- **"Cut full and roomy... when you get them, they stay that way"** — compensating for residual shrinkage from pre-Sanforization treatment
- **"Fast color indigo blue"** — indigo stability called out as a selling point
- **Work shirts**: "Good weight, soil resisting woven cotton chambray. Engineer Chambray" (Sears proprietary brand)
