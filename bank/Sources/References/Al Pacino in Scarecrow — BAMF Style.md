## Summary
Detailed costume breakdown of Al Pacino's Francis "Lion" Delbuchi in the 1973 film Scarecrow. Identifies the CPO shirt specifically, explains costume designer Jo Ynocencio's choices, and contextualises the garment's naval meaning within the character's backstory.

## Key Claims
- Pacino wore a navy woolen flannel CPO shirt: "dark-blue flannel cloth, long-pointed spread collar, six-button front placket, single-button cuffs, and patch pockets over the chest with gently pointed flap" closures
- Character Lion Delbuchi spent five years at sea — CPO shirt explicitly chosen to reflect his maritime background
- Costume designer: Jo Ynocencio (also dressed Pacino in The Panic in Needle Park)
- CPO shirt worn alongside chambray work shirt and knit balaclava to establish "weathered drifter aesthetic"
- The film is set in 1972; the article notes CPO shirt popularity "peaked in the '60s and '70s"
- The CPO shirt exemplified post-WWII servicemen repurposing military gear into civilian life
- Navy-issued CPO shirts derived from 1941 uniform regulations, prescribed as "blue flannel shirts" for chief petty officers [single-source — needs corroboration]
- Original 1939 "1st Model" had single left-side pocket; second pocket added in the 1940s
