## Summary
The 1930s as the golden age of men's formal dressing. Double-breasted and white summer versions emerged. Mass-produced ready-to-wear tuxedos appeared. Tuxedo rental became available. The American prom popularised in the 1930s.

## Key Claims
- 1930s widely regarded as peak of men's formal dressing
- DB and white summer versions emerged
- S. Rudofker's Sons (Philadelphia) — mass-produced ready-to-wear tuxedos
- Tuxedo rental became an option
- American prom descended from debutante balls, popularised in 1930s
