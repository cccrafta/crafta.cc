## Summary
Aggregated findings from 2025-2026 Harrington jacket buying guides covering contemporary makers across price points, from affordable to luxury. Documents who makes quality Harringtons today beyond Baracuta.

## Key Claims

### Heritage British Brands
- **Baracuta**: Still considered gold standard, "the originator of the Harrington jacket" — G9 model "serves as a reference point that most brands are riffing on to this day"
- **Fred Perry**: Slimmer, more streamlined silhouette with iconic laurel-wreath branding on breast
- **Aquascutum**: Technical cotton fabrics, slightly more formal feel and tailored execution — "every bit as good as Baracuta, if not better" (per Ape to Gentleman)
- **Burberry**: More refined take with cleaner lines, sharper fabrics, occasional flash of signature check lining
- **Private White V.C.**: Made in Manchester near original Baracuta location, premium materials, meticulous construction, elevated finish

### Affordable Options
- **Marks & Spencer**: Classic styling, wallet-friendly prices
- **Uniqlo**: Great value in navy, beige, olive green, red with chequered interior
- **Ralph Lauren**: Can be found on sale for under $200, includes throat-tab closure and tattersall touch

### Luxury Options
- **Tom Ford**: Most luxurious option, durable black nylon with smooth leather trim

### Other Notable Makers
- **Albam**: Contemporary styling using linen blend or cotton canvas, signature off-white zip tape, welted hand-warmer pockets
- **Boncoura** (Osaka, Japan): Founded 2011 by Hisashi Morishima, vintage-inspired reproduction with custom Japanese naturally over-dyed cotton/nylon fabric, meticulous attention to vintage details

### Production Notes
- **Baracuta current production**: Made in England and Europe (including Italy) depending on specific style
- **C.O.F. Studio**: Swedish-based (not Japanese), works with manufacturers in Japan, Italy, Portugal
