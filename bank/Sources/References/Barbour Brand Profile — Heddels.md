## Summary
Comprehensive Barbour brand profile. Covers 1894 founding in South Shields, paraffin wax innovation (replacing linseed oil), motorcycle heritage (1936 International suit), Dame Margaret Barbour's 1980 Bedale/Beaufort/Border designs, factory production details (160 parts, 36 people per jacket), and re-waxing service (13,000 jackets/year).

## Key Claims
- Founded 1894 by John Barbour in South Shields as oilcloth importer
- Perfected paraffin wax process — previous linseed oil cracked at low temperatures
- 1936: International motorcycle suit for Six Day Trials — worn by virtually every British team 1936-1977
- Steve McQueen wore the International
- 1980: Dame Margaret Barbour designed the Bedale, Beaufort, and Border after observing French jacket styles
- Bedale: 6oz waxed canvas, equestrian, short length, dual side vents
- Beaufort: raglan sleeves for shooting mobility, rear game pocket
- 160 parts per Bedale jacket. 36 people to make one. ~3,000/week produced.
- Three Royal Warrants: Duke of Edinburgh (1974), Queen Elizabeth II (1982), Prince of Wales (1987)
- 13,000 jackets re-waxed annually at South Shields factory
- Queen Elizabeth kept her Beaufort for 25 years, refusing replacements
