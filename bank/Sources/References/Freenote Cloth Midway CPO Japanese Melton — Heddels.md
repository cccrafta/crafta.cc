## Summary
Heddels coverage of Freenote Cloth's Midway CPO, noting Japanese melton wool sourcing, lighter-weight construction, and the design's M42 HBT inspiration — a distinction from strictly CPO reproductions.

## Key Claims
- Fabric: 10 oz. yarn-dyed melton wool blend from Japan; composition 70% wool / 20% polyester / 5% nylon / 5% acrylic
- Design inspired by the M42 HBT uniform shirt (not a strict CPO reproduction) — "one of the most commonly issued uniforms for American soldiers in WWII"
- Boxy fit and oversized pockets described as "practical necessities of a functional wartime garment"
- Construction: two expandable chest pockets, French placket, single needle stitch, double needle at stress points, custom horn buttons from Italy
- Cut and sewn in USA; retails at $400
- At 10 oz., this is among the lightest "CPO-adjacent" garments in the contemporary market — significantly below the 20-26 oz. military-spec melton range
