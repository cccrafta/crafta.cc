## Summary
Heddels Textile Tales deep dive on duck canvas — etymology, weave construction, weight grading system, and historical applications.

## Key Claims
- "Duck" from Dutch doek meaning "cloth" — originally linen canvas for sailors' trousers
- Cotton duck is heavy, plain woven cotton — differs from denim in being plain weave not twill
- Duck graded 1 (heaviest, 30 oz/sq yd) to 12 (lightest, 11.5 oz/sq yd) — system established in 1920s
- Single filled: single threads both crosswise and lengthwise; double fill: two threads twisted both ways
- Cotton duck strips were origin of duck tape, recorded in OED from 1899
- Canvas derives from 13th-century Anglo-French canevas, from Latin cannapaceus, from Greek kannabis (hemp)
- Ancient India began weaving cotton into canvas around 1500 BCE
