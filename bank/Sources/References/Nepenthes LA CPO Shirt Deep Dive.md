## Summary
Editorial deep dive on the CPO shirt jacket from Nepenthes LA, noting film appearances, historical evolution, and Engineered Garments' ongoing interpretations. One of the more focused cultural pieces on the garment.

## Key Claims
- 1930s: U.S. Navy issued CPO shirts to Chief Petty Officers as cold-weather layering
- Original construction: dense navy wool flannel, single scalloped chest pocket, straight hem
- 1940s: design shifted to two-pocket configuration with optional longer body for improved deck coverage [single-source — check against Heddels]
- Post-WWII: surplus stock and private reproductions transitioned the garment from naval to civilian use
- Al Pacino wore CPO shirt in Scarecrow (1973) — garment visible in the film and contextualised by character's naval background
- "The CPO shirt migrated from ship to city becoming a symbol of practical American dress, quietly linking military and civilian wardrobes"
- Engineered Garments: nearly two decades of CPO revisitations; FW25 fabrics include Dark Navy Heavy Wool Elastique, Wool Striped HB, corduroy versions
- EG CPO departs from original with snap placket, asymmetrical chest pockets, zip pockets on sleeves, curved hem
