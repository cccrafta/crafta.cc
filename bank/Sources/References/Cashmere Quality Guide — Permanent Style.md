## Summary
Two Permanent Style guides synthesized: the 2018 guide to cashmere and precious fibres, and the 2021 knitwear fibre guide. Combined, the most detailed T2 source on cashmere quality, construction, and practical assessment.

## Key Claims
- Best cashmere comes from Mongolia; cheaper versions from other parts of China, Iran, Afghanistan — very hard to tell apart when selecting cloth [single-source caveat: PS opinion]
- For durability in woven cashmere, seek twill weave over hopsack or plain, at weight of 340g/11oz or above
- Chinese cashmere: ~15–16 microns; Mongolian: 16.2–18 microns (note: some Mongolian grades coarser than Chinese due to breed variation)
- Cashmere has fewer scales in fiber structure than wool — this is why it's softer
- Cheap cashmere is knitted loosely so less yarn required; chemical softener added to feel softer; very fluffy but not strong
- Four-ply cashmere is a quality virtually no one sells anymore — warmer, heavier, dramatically more durable
- Cashmere crewneck documented as most popular PS product of the year (2024 pre-order)
- Cifonelli cashmere overcoat after 9 years: no balding, but "frustratingly soft" — rarely holds a clean line; author would not choose cashmere for a coat again
- Cashmere cardigan from 2009 (gift from Michael Drake): after 15+ years sent to Cashmere Circle for revive (washing, de-pilling, repair) at ~£87 — then looked new
- For longevity: a well-cared-for cashmere garment is repairable indefinitely
