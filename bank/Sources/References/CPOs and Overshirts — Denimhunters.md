## Summary
Comprehensive buying guide covering CPO history, melton wool properties, and specific fabric weights across six reviewed makers. Provides the most detailed weight comparison of any known CPO article.

## Key Claims
- CPO "first made its appearance on the decks of US Navy ships in the 1930s" issued to Chief Petty Officers
- Melton wool: "a tightly woven and neatly finished woollen fabric" historically used in English fox hunting jackets; found in peacoats, CPOs, and robust outerwear
- Real McCoy's CPO: 100% melton wool, made in Japan
- Dehen 1920 Crissman: 14 oz. melton wool with 100% cotton accent liner, milled in UK, made in USA
- Filson Mackinaw Jac-Shirt: 26 oz. 100% virgin Mackinaw Wool with 8 oz. cotton flannel lining; "100+ hours before this made-to-work classic" fully breaks in
- Pendleton Heston: 100% 23 oz. virgin wool, milled and constructed in Oregon or Washington
- Pike Brothers 1943 CPO: 80% wool / 20% polyamide middleweight blend, made in Portugal, based on original 1930s-era design
- Quality CPOs feature flat-felled seams throughout, heavy-duty buttons or snaps, and fabric that feels "stiff at first (will relax with wear)"
- Imogene + Willie Hall: 51% wool / 32% cotton / 17% recycled nylon double-faced blend, made in USA
