## Summary
Comprehensive Style 101 guide covering the safari jacket's military origins, Hollywood adoption, and modern interpretations. Includes "who wore it well" feature.

## Key Claims
- Term "safari jacket" first appeared mid-1930s, from British Khaki Drill uniforms introduced 1900 during Second Boer War
- Hemingway traveled to Africa 1933, had jackets made by Abercrombie & Fitch; their 1939 version described as "imported English, cotton drill material"
- Douglas Fairbanks Jr. wore a safari jacket with cravat in the 1940 film Safari
- Clark Gable wore safari attire in Mogambo (1953) alongside Grace Kelly
- YSL released African-themed collection with safari jackets in 1968, dressing Betty Cartroux and Loulou de la Falaise
- Sir Roger Moore wore safari clothing in five Bond films plus The Persuaders (1971) and The Sea Wolves (1980)
- Prince Charles photographed in stone safari jacket in Papua New Guinea, 1984
- Anderson & Sheppard produces a safari jacket with 15 pockets
