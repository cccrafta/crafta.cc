## Summary
Community memory article documenting civilian CPO shirt/jacket culture in the 1970s, with specific retail channels, prices, and descriptions of who wore them. Useful as qualitative evidence of peak civilian adoption.

## Key Claims
- Peak civilian popularity: 1972-1974, though the trend began late 1960s and extended into early 1980s
- Primary wearers: school-age boys and teenagers in junior and senior high school — "all the boys wore them"
- Colors available at mass retail: dark navy blue (authentic military), dark green, brown, tan, various plaid patterns (green/white, brown/cream, red/orange)
- Fabric: heavy wool (often described as itchy), cotton flannel, some quilted-lined versions
- Features noted: four front pockets with flaps, anchor-emblazoned black buttons, pointed collars, buttoned cuffs, unlined or partially satin-lined
- Retail price circa 1968: approximately $20 at F.W. Woolworth's
- Retail channels: J.C. Penney, Montgomery Ward, Sears, Woolworth's, Army-Navy surplus stores, E.J. Korvettes, Kohl's
- The garment straddled authentic surplus and mass-market imitation — key to its broad civilian reach
