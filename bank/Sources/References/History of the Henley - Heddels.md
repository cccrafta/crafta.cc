## Summary
Comprehensive history of the henley shirt from 19th-century English undervests through its adoption as rowing sportswear at Henley-on-Thames (Royal Regatta since 1839), its role as precursor to the modern t-shirt, and its revival as a heritage fashion staple.

## Key Claims
- Henleys first appeared as 19th-century English undergarments called "undervests"
- Named after Henley-on-Thames and its Royal Regatta (established 1839)
- Cooper Underwear Co. (~1904) simplified the henley into the crew-neck t-shirt for single men
- Ralph Lauren buyer discovered vintage henley in the 1970s, catalyzing its fashion revival
- Merz b. Schwanen revived historical German knitwear starting from a vintage henley reproduction
