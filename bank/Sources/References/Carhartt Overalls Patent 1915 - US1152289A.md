## Summary
Patent filed November 20, 1914, granted August 31, 1915. Inventors: Hamilton Carhartt, Hamilton Carhartt Jr., and Oscar E. Kratz, all of Detroit, Michigan. Assignee: Hamilton Carhartt Manufacturer. Documents Carhartt's specific reinforcement system for workmen's overalls — their own engineering documentation in their own words.

## Key Claims
- **Problem**: Reinforce "points commonly under the greatest strain in use and where such garments most frequently rip or tear, or wear out"
- **Continuous facing strip** (component 11) bound around the entire bib edge, from one side opening to the other
- **Double-row stitching** using "a two-needle sewing machine" — two parallel stitch lines for the facing
- **Reinforced buttonholes** positioned within the facing strip at stress points
- **Front pockets** (15) secured under the facing strips at side seams
- **Hip pockets** (21) reinforced through facing strips
- **Pencil pocket** (28) as a specific feature
- **Carpenter's rule pocket** (30) — specifically designed for carpenters
- **Rear waistband** reinforced with doubled straps for suspender button support
- Filed 1914, two years before Hamilton Carhartt's death in 1917
