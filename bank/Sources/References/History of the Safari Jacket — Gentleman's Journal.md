## Summary
Overview of the safari jacket's history from the Boer War through Hollywood to Prince Charles, with buying recommendations for Savile Row linen versions.

## Key Claims
- Safari jacket originated during the Second Boer War (turn of 20th century) when British troops needed heat-resistant uniforms
- Hemingway created a bespoke bush jacket in 1936 with Willis & Geiger
- "Safari suit" added to the Oxford English Dictionary approximately 1935
- By 1939, Abercrombie & Fitch began selling safari jackets wholesale
- Clark Gable wore a safari jacket in the 1950s film Mogambo
- Willis & Geiger also dressed Charles Lindbergh, Amelia Earhart, and Sir Edmund Hillary
- Prince Charles wore a sand-coloured safari suit during an Australian tour to Ayers Rock (Octopussy era, 1983)
