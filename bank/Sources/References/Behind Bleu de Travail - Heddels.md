## Summary
History of bleu de travail (French work blues). Covers moleskin as the primary fabric, color coding by trade (black for carpenters, white for painters, blue for mechanics), and the cultural transition from workwear to fashion icon.

## Key Claims
- The depth of blue comes from moleskin having both warp and weft dyed the same color
- 1844: Adolphe Lafont created first standardized bleu de travail
- Color coding: black (carpenters), white (painters), blue (mechanics/drivers)
- Bill Cunningham made the French chore coat famous in America
- West German Army used dyed moleskin for military uniforms
- Moleskin sometimes called "French denim" — misnomer since denim is also French
