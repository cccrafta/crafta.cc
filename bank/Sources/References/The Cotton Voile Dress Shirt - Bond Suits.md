## Summary
Deep technical profile of voile as a dress shirt fabric, tracing its use across the Bond film series from Connery through Craig. Covers construction, the doubled-front solution for sheerness, and the Jermyn Street makers behind the shirts.

## Key Claims
- Alan Flusser defines voile as "woven from fine hand twisted yarns with reverse twist warp threads, this plain fabric is lightweight, cool, and dry"
- Voile differs from poplin in that sheerness is mandatory for voile regardless of weight; poplin is only sheer in lighter weights
- The doubled front solves sheerness: marcella bib or pleated panel over voile body — the dinner jacket covers the sheer sides and back
- Bond voile shirt appearances: Sean Connery (*Dr. No*, 1962), George Lazenby (*On Her Majesty's Secret Service*, 1969), Roger Moore (*Moonraker*, *Octopussy*, *A View to a Kill*), Daniel Craig (*Skyfall*, 2012)
- Makers: Turnbull & Asser, Frank Foster, Tom Ford, Lanvin
