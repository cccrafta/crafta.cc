## Summary
Comprehensive history of the Harrington jacket from its Manchester origins as the Baracuta G9 golf jacket through its adoption by British and American subcultures. Covers the Miller Brothers' creation, technical details, naming origin via Peyton Place, and cultural significance.

## Key Claims
- **Baracuta founded mid-1930s** by John and Isaac Miller in Manchester, initially producing rain coats for Burberry and Aquascutum
- **G9 designed 1937** specifically for golf, with shorter fit than traditional rain coats to improve swing mobility
- **Fraser tartan lining approved 1938** by Lord Lovat (24th Chieftain of the Scottish Fraser Clan)
- **Manchester produced 8 billion yards of cloth in 1912**, earning nickname "Cottonopolis"
- **James Dean wore bright red Harrington** (by Bud Berma, not Baracuta) in *Rebel Without a Cause* (1956)
- **Elvis Presley wore Baracuta G9** in *King Creole* (1958)
- **"Harrington" name originated 1966** when British menswear specialist John Simons marketed the jacket after Rodney Harrington character from *Peyton Place*
- **McGregor released "Drizzler" 1947** as competing design with point collar instead of stand collar
- **Technical specs**: water-resistant poplin, ventilated rear yoke, elasticated cuffs and waistband, zip closure, short fit above waistline
- **Bob Hope and Bing Crosby** wore G9 for golf by late 1940s in America
- **Steve McQueen wore navy G9** in *The Thomas Crown Affair* (1968)
- **Japanese adoption**: called "Swing jacket" or "Swing Top" due to golfing heritage
- **Paul Weller quote**: "one of the most iconic jackets ever designed"
