## Summary
Aggregated historical facts from primary and secondary sources about the Baracuta G9's creation, naming, and technical construction. Includes details from Baracuta's own archives and specialist menswear publications.

## Key Claims
- **G9 name meaning**: 'G' stands for Golf, '9' represents the nine holes of a golf course
- **Founded 1937 by John and Isaac Miller** (note: some sources say "brothers," others say "John and Isaac" without specifying relationship — appears to be brothers based on multiple sources)
- **Fraser Tartan approval 1938** by Brigadier Simon Christopher Joseph Fraser, 25th Clan Chief of Clan Fraser of Lovat (note: some sources say 24th, others 25th Chieftain — may refer to different numbering systems)
- **Fraser Tartan has 13th-century Scottish-Gaelic roots** — the pattern and color have never changed since 1938
- **G4 model created 1944** as more relaxed alternative with button cuffs and open waist (vs elasticated cuffs/waist on G9)
- **Umbrella back yoke** design inspired by umbrella structure to shed rainwater
- **Raglan sleeves** allowed full golf swing motion
- **Deep front pockets** designed to hold golf balls
- **Two-button dog ear collar** as signature feature
- **Original fabric** was high-thread-count cotton before polyester invention; modern versions use 50/50 poly-cotton blend with Coolmax tartan lining
- **Ryan O'Neal as Rodney Harrington** in *Peyton Place* (1964-1969) wore G9 frequently on-screen
- **John Simons connection**: Opened The Ivy Shop in Richmond in 1964; in 1965 (not 1966 as some sources claim) placed hand-written note in window calling Baracuta G9 the "Rodney Harrington" jacket — this is first documented use of "Harrington" as generic term
- **Frank Sinatra** wore Baracuta in *Assault on a Queen* (1966)
- **2007 70th anniversary edition** featured special packaging with quotes from Elvis, McQueen, and Sinatra printed on lining
