## Summary
History of Hawick as the global center of cashmere knitwear production from the late 18th century, covering the industrial development, rise of cashmere, and contemporary decline and survival.

## Key Claims
- Hawick organised hosiery trade dates to 1771; John Hardie introduced first stocking frames
- By the 1870s, 250 woollen mills concentrated around Hawick and Scottish Borders — over 40% of Scotland's factory labour force
- 1870s: Yorkshire mill-worker Joseph Dawson invents efficient mechanical de-hairing process for cashmere, inspired by overland journey through India; revolutionizes industrial production
- Dawson's company later acquired Pringle of Scotland and Ballantyne
- John Laing (founded 1831) is the oldest cashmere knitwear company in the world
- Pringle of Scotland founded 1815 by Robert Pringle; began as undergarment manufacturer
- Pringle's Hawick plant shuttered 2008
- Caerlee Mill (Ballantyne) shuttered and demolished
- Chanel acquired Barrie Knitwear 2012 through Métiers d'Art initiative
- Key survivors: William Lockie (est. 1874), Johnstons of Elgin (est. 1797), Barrie Knitwear (est. 1903)
