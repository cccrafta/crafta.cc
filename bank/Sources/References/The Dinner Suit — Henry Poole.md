## Summary
Henry Poole & Co's own account of inventing the dinner suit in 1865 — the primary source for the garment's origin.

## Key Claims
- 1865: Prince of Wales (future Edward VII) commissioned Henry Poole to create "a short coat he could wear at informal dinners at Sandringham"
- Presented to the Prince in celestial blue; no earlier reference in Poole ledgers dating to 1846
- British dinner jacket became known as "tuxedo" after Tuxedo Park Club, north of New York City
- Four Tuxedo Club founders — William Waldorf Astor, Robert Goelet, Ogden Mills, Pierre Lorillard — documented as Poole customers in the 1860s
- Dinner jacket likely introduced to New York society ~20 years before the apocryphal James Potter incident
- Henry Poole died in 1876, never seeing his design called a dinner jacket or cross the Atlantic
